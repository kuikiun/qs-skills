# ANZSMM 2018 — Structure (§12–15)

## Strict application — no inferring or guessing

When applying this skill to QS work:

- Apply **only** rules explicitly stated in this skill or in the cited source standard.
- Do **not** infer, interpolate, or guess rules that aren't explicit.
- If a situation isn't covered by an explicit rule, **stop and flag it** to the user — don't assume by analogy, "common practice," or what seems obvious.
- Quote the rule number (e.g. `M3`, `P1`, `D2`) when applying a rule so the user can verify against the source.
- If contract documents conflict with the standard, contract documents prevail (per the standard's own override rules) — but state the deviation explicitly in the BOQ / cost plan / cost analysis output for the user's review.
- For anything not covered by an explicit rule, refer the user to the source PDF and ask which rule to apply — do not make one up.

This directive applies to every skill in qs-skills.

## §12 MASONRY

**Items given:**
- (a) Samples
- (b) Sample panels
- (c) Tests

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: Measure deductions for strings, sills, lintels, corbels and similar items.
- **M2**: Calculate opening sizes to **include** sills, lintels, etc. where they occur.
- **M3**: Give particulars of bricks, blocks, cement, lime, sand, composition and mix.
- **M4**: Classify by size, colour, texture, course height, bond/pattern; whether hollow, screen, solid or other type of brick/block.
- **M5**: Measure common brickwork, fairface on common brickwork, and facing brickwork **separately**.
- **M6**: Facings — to two full courses below finished ground level (unless contract documents specify otherwise).
- **M7**: Describe pointing to one or both faces as required.
- **M8**: Measure separately purpose-made bricks/blocks; give immediately following the work in which they occur.
- **M9**: Classify by levels; group consecutive similar floors.
- **M10**: Measure separately work documented as executed **overhand**.

## §13 STONEWORK

**Particulars given:**
- (a) Stone, cement, lime, sand, composition and mix of mortar
- (b) Workmanship

**Items given:**
- (a) Samples
- (b) Tests
- (c) Extra hoisting for all stone >6m above ground in increments of 6m

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: Coursed work — state height of courses; if regularly diminished, describe and state min/max.
- **M2**: Give nature of face finish (incl. rustication, type of pointing, joint thickness; state if pointing material differs from body mortar).
- **M3**: Chases, caulking, wall reinforcement, stays/straps, air bricks, flue linings, parging, DPCs, ant capping → per §12 Masonry.
- **M4**: ≤500mm thick in m²; >500mm thick in m³.
- **M5**: State if stone set on natural bed.
- **M6**: Stones >2m in length or height — measure separately, state dimensions.
- **M7**: Stones >1m³ content — in 0.25m³ increments.
- **M8**: Stones in m³ measured from smallest rectangular cube from which they can be obtained.
- **M9**: Circular work includes templates; state if back of stonework cut circular or left straight.

**Deemed included (P1):**
All joggles, cramps, dowels, ties, templates, plumbing angles, setting, jointing, forming cavities, pointing, hoisting ≤6m above ground.

**D1**: Stonework covers all types of masonry (granite, marble, slate).

### §13.1 Natural Stonework — classification highlights:
- Footings (m²/m³ stating thickness)
- Walls / wall linings (m², state thickness; M2 face area in m² when given in m³); skins of hollow walls; facings to concrete/brickwork/other; tapered/battering/circular; reveals of openings; ends of walls
- Quoins (plain/sunk/moulded/circular sunk/circular circular/circular sunk/similar) — m² / m³
- Pilasters (incl. sunk to entasis), jambs, caps & bases, springers, voussoirs, keystones, columns (incl. sunk to entasis), fireplaces & chimney stacks — no/m³
- Isolated/attached piers — m (≤500mm wide) / m² (>500mm wide)
- Plinths, string courses, corbelling — m or m²
- Lintels, arches (state wall thickness, rise from springing, including centering. M8: arch stones with quarry bed horizontal — measure blocks sufficiently large)
- Flush/recessed/projecting quoins, bands, string courses, cornices — m
- Flush/recessed/projecting panels — no / m² (state no. of panels)
- Landings, treads, risers, steps, hearths, kerbs, fenders, window sills, borders, margins, shelving — m / m² (state size, lengths >2m, exposed ends)
- Architraves, archivolts, copings, templates, kneelers, bonders, apex stones — m / no
- Polishing (D2: polishing after completion)
- Irregular/splayed/rounded angles — m
- Levelling up in random walling (D4)
- Forming straight vertical joints (D5)
- Special metal supporting brackets, shelf angles
- Mullions, transoms, reveal linings, stoolings, balusters, finials, crockets, terminals — no / m
- Exposed edges, chamfers, splays, grooves, flutings — m / no
- Stonework checked, sunk or specially cut (D6: to fit steel/concrete)
- Running ornaments (bead and reel, egg and dart, dentil courses) — m
- Carving / sculpture / boasting / mouldings up to carving (refer to details/spec)
- Pavings (m², state thickness)
- Skirtings, coves (m, state size)

### §13.2 Artificial Stone, Terracotta and Similar Works
- Artificial stone, terracotta, similar (m²)
- Including all moulds, reinforcement, filling voids with concrete where applicable
- M1: Otherwise describe as for natural stonework, similar manner to §10 Precast Concrete

## §14 STRUCTURAL STEEL

**Items given:**
- (a) Steel — type and quality, quoting standard spec
- (b) Workmanship — relevant spec clauses, standard specs
- (c) Preparation of shop drawings
- (d) All necessary temporary bracing for erection
- (e) Samples, (f) tests
- (g) Inspection of welds (incl. special techniques)
- (h) Special trial shop assembly

**Heaviest lift / highest lift identification:**
- §14-5: State heaviest lift and its height above/depth below ground level; identify item with general description.
- §14-6: State highest lift and mass; identify with general description.

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: Make **NO deductions** of mass for drillings, holes, notchings, similar.
- **M2**: Mass = net theoretical mass with no allowance for rolling margin or welding materials.
- **M3**: Steelwork to existing buildings or between existing walls/structures — measure separately, include all available info.
- **M4**: Welded, bolted, riveted work — measure separately.
- **M5**: Cranked/irregular shape/precambered members — measure separately.
- **M6**: All steel sections and plates measured in **multiples of 0.1m** in length for mass calculation.
- **M7**: Classify by levels; group consecutive similar floors.
- **M8**: Give columns separately between splice points if splice points don't coincide with height stages.
- **M11**: Attached connections / fitted stiffeners → measure immediately following the group/classification to which attached (e.g. columns, beams, rafters, purlins).
- **M12**: Measure separately attached base plates, cap plates, butt plates, similar.
- **M13/M16**: Patented or standard proprietary items — describe fully and give by no. or m as appropriate.
- **M19**: Standard proprietary purlins, girts, accessories — describe fully.
- **M20**: Bolt length = total thickness of members + diameter + 5mm (+ extra 5mm if bevelled washers required).
- **M22**: Wrapping — measure net area.
- **M23**: Surface treatments — separate item per classification; include "item" under each for surface treatment to attached plates and loose connections.
- **M24**: Surface treatments in m or by no. according to description.
- **M26/M27**: Do **NOT** measure shop drillings for fixings, nor drillings for carpenter's bolts securing timbers (where shown on steelwork drawings).

**Classification & units:**

| Class | Units | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1–6. Columns / beams / rafters / purlins / girts / bracing | t | Boxed / compound / composite / built-up / plain (angles, channels, UB, hollow sections, similar). State type, size, no. and lengths |
| 7. Temporary bracing | t / item | State nature of bracing. D2: wall bracing between columns, top/bottom chord bracing between trusses. D3: erection-only bracing |
| 8. Attached connections and fitted stiffeners | item | State no. (optional), size. D4: includes all sections/plates in stiffeners, cleats, brackets, gussets, packers, coaming plates, around holes/openings, bearing/end plates, splice plates |
| 9. Loose connections (bearing plates built into walls, similar) | no | D5: includes loose splice, cleats, brackets, separators, distance pieces, similar unattached items |
| 10–14. Rigid frames / trusses / lattice girders / open web joists / verandahs and awnings | t | State size and type, max dimensions, sizes of sections, plate thicknesses, no. of frames |
| Verandahs / awnings / suspension rods / turnbuckles | no | Including bolts, total mass; in pairs |
| 15. Staircases | t | State design particulars, no. of flights, sizes, no. of landings, treads, risers |
| Cranking | no | M17: alternatively state no. of cranks in description |
| 16. Rails (rails, standard fish plates, specially designed fish plates, hook bolts, dog spikes, special rail clips) | t / no | State mass per metre, length between cranks |
| 17. Standard proprietary purlins, girts, accessories (purlins/girts/bridging/tie rods) | m / no | State nature/centres of supports, no. of units, including drillings/laps/splices/fixings, bolts, nuts, washers. D6: excludes proprietary girts in metal framed partitions |
| 18. Bolts (holding down / in connections / attachments) | no | State length in 100mm increments, material, type, classification |
| 19. Holding down bolt assemblies | no | State no., diameter, size; including casting/building in |
| 20. Shear studs / 21. ties (shop/field; masonry/similar) / 22. wrapping (wire/fabric/woven wire strip) | no / t / m² / m | State diameter, type, extent of laps, fixing method. D7 (bolts other than black bolts in connections), D8 (ties shop welded), D9 (wrapping where steelwork encased) |
| 23. Wedges and grouting (under base/bearing plates, grillages) | m² | State thickness, type; including wedging, levelling; state no. of plates |
| 24. Surface treatments (shop priming, phosphating, hot dip galvanising, metal spraying, special, fire rating) | t / m | State type, member thickness, bolt diameter |
| 25. Field work / 26. holes, sinkings (holes, cutting, notching, similar; welding; riveting; ties welded; ductwork) | no / m | M25: separately according to description |

**Deemed included (P1):**
All shop fabrication, marking, drillings for bolts (including those securing timbers as shown), black bolts, nuts and any type of washer, riveted work, counter sinking, tapping for bolts/screws.

**P2 (framed structures, additional):**
Cutting to dead lengths, notching and machining ends and bearings.

## §15 METALWORK

**Items given:** (a) shop drawings, (b) samples, (c) tests.

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: State kind, finish and quality of metal — group as steel, stainless steel, aluminium, copper, brass, bronze, cast iron, similar.
- **M2**: Describe type of fabrication (welded frames, bolted frames, etc.).
- **M3**: Describe work to be primed or painted before delivery to site.
- **M4**: Describe surface treated (other than by painting).
- **M5**: Include particulars of fixing method, nature of background/support.
- **M6**: Painting per §29.
- **M7**: Sheet metal coverings, frames, etc. — measure in m stating width where ≤250mm wide (except where caused by openings).
- **M10**: Tie rods/bolts/fixings — give following the items of work to be fixed.
- **M11**: Fixings may be in description of item where item to be fixed is given by no.

## When to apply this skill

Apply when measuring or pricing: bricks, blocks, masonry walls/piers/arches, glass blocks, DPCs, ant caps; natural or artificial stone (walls, facings, copings, sills, treads, ornaments, mouldings); structural steel framing (columns, beams, rafters, purlins, bracing, trusses, frames, staircases, rails); proprietary purlins and girts; bolts, holding-down bolts, shear studs; surface treatments (galvanising, priming, fire rating); architectural metalwork (gratings, covers, balustrades, handrails, mesh, shelving, brackets, sheet metal cladding).
