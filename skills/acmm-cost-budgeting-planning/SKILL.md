The Australian Cost Management Manual (ACMM) is the AIQS-published methodology for managing project cost from brief through final account. Volume 1 §2 covers everything **before** the contract is let.

## §2.1 Cost Budgeting — purpose

A method of arriving at the sum within which the project will be cost-planned. Budgets are based on:

- Historical **elemental** costs or **functional** costs (Section 5)
- Rates current at a stated **base date**
- Rates valid for a **Locality Index of 100** (metropolitan) — adjusted for other localities
- Rates that **exclude**: Alterations, Siteworks, External Services, External Alterations, Special Provisions (treated as **Project Specifics**), and Centralised Energy (its own functional area)
- Rates that exclude items not in a normal building contract (e.g. loose furniture)
- Rates that allow for **normal storey height** for the building type (variations attract Project Specifics)

## Project Specifics

Items varying project-to-project, costed individually over and above functional area pricing:

| Category | Items |
|---|---|
| Excluded from elemental rates | (a) Internal/external alterations, (b) siteworks, (c) external services, (d) special provisions (contingencies), (e) temporary work |
| Special factors | Site conditions (abnormal footings, earthquake), climatic, evaporative cooling/AC if not in FA rates, height other than normal, extreme project size, improved standards, non-standard procurement (D&C, fast-track) |
| Indices | Time (BCI), locality (LI) |

## Budgeting procedures

| Stage | Responsibility | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Inception / overall program | Client Authority | Simple order-of-magnitude budget |
| Brief — Stage **A** | Project Design Team | **Initial Budget** — functional units OR FA per m² OR element area per m² |
| Outline Proposals — Stage **B** | Project Design Team | Optional — comparative costs for several outline proposals |
| Sketch Design — Stage **C** | Project Design Team | **Final Budget** — measured FAs × m² rates; estimated services elementally |

### Initial Budget cost build-up
1. Main building Functional Areas (FAs) — functional unit cost OR FA per m² OR element area per m² rates
2. Travel/Engineering Areas — % addition to total FA
3. Lifts (other than intra-FA) — provisional allowance
4. Ancillary buildings — separate budget items (m² rates, or as Project Specifics)
5. Centralised Energy (service + housing) — provisional allowance
6. Update by Building Cost Index (BCI)
7. Siteworks and external services — historical data
8. Special factors and other Project Specifics

### Final Budget cost build-up
Same as Initial but with:
- Recommended FAs **tested by design**: FAs measured from drawings × m² rates
- Travel/Engineering Areas measured from drawings (verify they don't exceed allowance)
- Lifts realistically estimated
- Centralised Energy separately estimated
- Siteworks separately estimated by measurement

### Indices and contingencies
Budgets include:
- (a) BCI update to date of budget preparation
- (b) Locality index adjustment (unless special conditions apply)
- (c) Contingency sum for unknowns

### Locality Indices applied to the **composite total** of Project Specifics, updated FAs, and Travel/Engineering Areas.

## §2.2 Cost Planning — purpose

Designing **to** a cost, not costing a design. The capital-cost discipline over the four stages:

| Stage | Trigger | Output | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Brief | Brief Stage Cost / Initial Budget | Establish initial budget for client; confirm feasibility |
| B | Outline Proposals | Outline Proposal Cost | Identify best means of satisfying brief; compare alternative outline proposals |
| C | Sketch Design | **Sketch Design Cost Plan** | Confirm/set final budget; establish elemental cost benchmarks |
| D | Documentation | **Tender Document Cost Plan** (interim cost plans if required) | Continuous cost checks during detail development; ensure design contained within budget |

### Stage C — Sketch Design Cost Plan
Information assumed available:
1. Final dimensioned sketch plans, elevations, sections
2. Structural sketches with member dimensions
3. Schedule of finishes
4. Site layout (contours, roads, paths, landscaping, services, covered ways, fencing)
5. Specification notes
6. Estimates for services broken down to elements per building + externally

Output: elemental cost plan with specific construction, finishes, services stated; elemental unit rates **and** quantities required.

### Stage D — Tender Document Cost Plan
Prepared from final working drawings + specification. Includes a **reconciliation statement** comparing each element's totals/areas with the Sketch Design Cost Plan (or Interim Cost Plan).

Cost monitoring during documentation: comparative cost studies, research, advice on detailing economics, alternative finishes/methods, monthly cost statements presented elementally.

### Cost checks during documentation
- On nothing less than a **complete element**
- Project Team Leader / Project Manager advised **immediately** of result
- If possible, deal first with the more cost-significant elements
- Project team meets at least monthly

### Reconciliation
The Interim and Tender Document Cost Plans must include a reconciliation statement comparing element totals/areas with the previous cost plan; **all differences fully reconciled**.

### Separate buildings
Where a project has more than one building, each is shown separately throughout. Buildings <200 m² GFA may (PM discretion) skip elemental form and be included in element XB-Outbuildings.

## Contingency allowances — four types

| Type | Purpose | Reduces to |
|---|---|---|
| **Planning** | Risk of not designing the spatial relationships and achieving desired Functional Area / Travel-Engineering allowances | Zero at Sketch Design |
| **Design** | Risk that estimator/cost planner doesn't adequately foresee correct design or complexity | Zero at Tender Document stage |
| **Contract** | Risk of variations and unforeseen items during construction | Remains through construction |
| **Project** | Delays and/or inflation, major changes by Client/authorities, fee negotiations | Remains, may be released |

## Cost limit / final budget rules

- Once established, the **final budget should not be exceeded** except for major Client/Authority changes or BCI changes (if permitted by Client's budgeting procedures)
- If exceeded, inform Project Team Leader / Project Manager **immediately** for remedial action
- All standard forms (in Appendix A of Vol 1) are used; copies forwarded to Client

## Cost plans must reflect

- **Building price** (includes market conditions), not building cost (which excludes market conditions)
- Every component of the project — if information unavailable, an allowance is determined and included; if at risk, attention drawn to it
- Allowances broken down **elementally / sub-elementally** (not lump sums) for electrical, mechanical, hydraulic
- Allowances explicit re. locality, time, design contingencies
