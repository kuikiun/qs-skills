# ANZSMM 2018 — General Rules

The Australian and New Zealand Standard Method of Measurement of Building Works (ANZSMM, 2018 Edition) is the joint trans-Tasman standard for measuring building works in Bills of Quantities (BOQ) — called Schedule of Quantities in NZ. It supersedes ASMM6 (Australia) and NZS 4202 (NZ).

This skill captures the foundational rules from §1 ("Introduction, General Rules and Recommendations"). All trade-specific skills (`anzsmm-substructure`, `anzsmm-structure`, etc.) inherit these rules.

## Purpose & scope

- Provides a uniform basis for measuring building works.
- Applies to BOQ preparation **before** works commence, AND to measurement of finished works and variations.
- BOQs must identify which version of ANZSMM was used.
- Rules in the document are over-ridden by particular construction details required by contract documents, or by country-specific legislation.

## Tender & document flow

- Tender documents must be in the QS's hands **seven clear days** before BOQs are issued to tenderers.
- A list of those documents must be included in the BOQ.

## Bills of Quantities — core principles

| Rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 2.1 | BOQ must identify which ANZSMM version was used |
| 2.2 | Adequately describe materials and workmanship; accurately represent quantities |
| 2.3 | If departing from ANZSMM rules, state the method adopted in the BOQ for each occurrence |
| 2.4 | Work that cannot be accurately measured → mark as **Provisional** and identify in BOQ |
| 2.5 | Work not covered by existing trade sections → measure under a new trade with a relevant heading |

## Measurement (§3)

- **3.1** Measure work **net as fixed in position**, unless stated otherwise.
- **3.2** Minimum deductions for voids/openings refer to:
  - (a) voids wholly within boundaries of measured areas;
  - (b) door, window and blank openings;
  - (c) voids at boundaries from projections of piers, columns and similar.
- **3.3** No deductions for:
  - voids less than **1 m²** (for items measured by area);
  - voids less than **0.1 m³** (for items measured by volume) — **except** walls and slabs given by volume, where the 1 m² rule applies.

## Description (§4)

- **4.1** Dimensions order: **length, width, height/depth** (unless ambiguous, then label them).
- **4.2** Give all dimensions, girths, thicknesses, mass, and other info needed for proper assessment.
- **4.3** Patented / standard proprietary / prefabricated items may be measured per the manufacturer's practice — state the manner adopted.
- **4.4** Where practicable, descriptions or group headings reference the general location in the works.
- **4.5** Unless stated, items are deemed to **include**:
  - (a) materials and goods (incl. lapping, jointing, conveyance, delivery, unloading, storing, returning packings, handling, hoisting, lowering);
  - (b) square, raking, splay and circular cutting;
  - (c) waste of materials;
  - (d) labour and all costs (drilling, setting, fitting, fixing in position);
  - (e) establishment costs, overhead costs and profit;
  - (f) plant and equipment;
  - (g) taxes, overtime, bonus and incentive payments, duties, royalties;
  - (h) protection of work against damage (except where specific protection means are stated → measure separately).
- **4.6** Forming of ends, angles, returned ends, junctions between straight/raking/circular work — **included** with the work in which they occur.
- **4.7** Descriptions may reference specification clauses or drawing numbers where helpful.

## Terminology (§5)

- **"given"** — info may appear in heading, sub-heading, preamble, or description.
- **"items will be given for"** — only required where contract documents demand them; additional items may also be given.
- Increments (e.g. "100mm") = "not exceeding 100mm wide; exceeding 100 and not exceeding 200mm wide; etc."
- "not exceeding [width]" — group all work up to that width; do not split by individual widths.
- "existing" — taken to have been carried out **prior** to any new work measured.
- Some terminology differs AU vs NZ → refer to **NZ Guidance Notes**.

## Billing units (§6)

| Unit | Rule |
|---|---|
| m, m², m³ | Bill to the **full unit** (any part of a unit = a full unit). Exception: high-value items <10 units → one decimal place. |
| t (tonne) | Bill to the full one-hundredth of a unit (parts <0.01 = 0.01). Express as decimals; place 0 before decimal point if no whole units. |

## Monetary sums (§7)

- Monetary sums and rates are designated in the BOQ within the **Included Sums** section (see §39).

## Nominated sub-contractors & suppliers (§8)

- **8.1** Attendance and general facilities for nominated sub-contractors → given as an item following each monetary sum, OR as integral part of each monetary rate. Associated construction work is measured per the appropriate Section.
- **8.2** Fixing materials/goods from nominated sub-contractors/suppliers → given per the relevant Section. If transport costs are borne by the contractor, state particulars.

## Temporary, remedial, restoration works (§9)

- Where documented, describe and measure in detail.

## Circular work (§10)

- Circular work (unless required later to state diameter) is described and given separately, classified as:
  - (a) radius ≤ 0.5 m
  - (b) > 0.5 m, ≤ 1 m
  - (c) > 1 m, ≤ 5 m
  - (d) > 5 m, ≤ 10 m
  - (e) > 10 m, in 10 m increments
- "Circular" includes any form of curve. Conical and spherical = describe and give separately.
- Irregular curved surfaces — give separately, stating min and max radii.

## Uncommon situations (§11)

- Work in uncommon situations (in/under water, compressed air, confined spaces) → describe fully with relevant spec clauses and drawings, measure separately.
- **Confined spaces** = working space less than **1.75 m** high.

## BOQ order (§12)

- Methods are grouped to suit the **NATSPEC** specification system.
- If project specification doesn't use NATSPEC, follow the project spec order and cross-reference the appropriate Section of ANZSMM in the BOQ.
- Each BOQ section, clause, item and note → individually and positively identified.

## Item order (§13)

- For items of similar description, order in **ascending order of size**.

## Complex work (§14)

- Items of a complex nature unable to be easily described may reference a detailed drawing and/or specification clause.

## How tabulated rules work (§15)

- Each section comprises **classification tables** and **supplementary rules** (Measurement Rules, Measurement and Prices, Definitions).
- **Horizontal lines** divide tables into zones with different rules.
- Everything above the horizontal line directly under a classification table heading applies throughout that table.
- Item descriptions must identify the work using descriptive features drawn from each applicable column of the classification table.
- **Bold** wording in sections has a corresponding entry in the **Definitions** column.

## Measurement and Prices clause (§16)

- Each BOQ section must contain a reference drawing attention to the Measurement and Prices clause in the relevant ANZSMM section.
- The standard Measurement and Prices clause is not repeated in the BOQ, but **any amendment must be stated**.

## When to apply this skill

- Drafting any BOQ — every trade section inherits these rules.
- Reviewing a BOQ for compliance with ANZSMM 2018.
- Resolving questions about whether work is "included" in an item or measured separately.
- Deciding billing units and rounding.
- Handling provisional work, circular work, uncommon situations, or work outside the standard trades.
