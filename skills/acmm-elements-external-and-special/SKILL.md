# ACMM Vol 2 — External, Alterations & Special Provisions (31–46)

This skill covers Alterations, Site Works, External Services, External Alterations and Special Provisions. The "X" prefix denotes external/site work (allocated at total-project level, not per building). The "Y" prefix denotes Special Provisions.

For Substructure/Superstructure see `acmm-elements-substructure-superstructure`. For finishes/fittings see `acmm-elements-finishes-fittings`. For services see `acmm-elements-services`.

---

## 31 AR — Alterations and Renovations

**Definition:** Costs for altering / renovating existing buildings within the project.

**INCLUDES:** demolition; protection; making good; new work in existing buildings (with elements coded normally); decoration; preparation of existing surfaces.

**EXCLUDES:** Internal/External Alterations is treated as a Project Specific (excluded from elemental and functional area rates per Vol 1 §2.1 — costed separately).

Note: AR is allocated to the building being altered, not to overall project. Use this element to roll up alteration cost in a single coded line for a multi-building project.

Sub-element divisions to be included as needed for the type of alteration work.

---

## ALTERATIONS — note on "Internal Alterations"

Internal Alterations are typically rolled to the AR element of the building they belong to. **External Alterations** sit in a separate top-level element (45 XX).

## SITE WORKS (32–36)

### 32 XP — Site Preparation

**Definition:** Site clearance and preparatory work to make site ready for construction.

**INCLUDES:** site clearance (vegetation, scrub, top soil stripping); reduced level excavation (XPRL); removal of existing structures/foundations; site grading; site fencing for construction; demolition (where this is a site preparation activity rather than alteration).

**Sub-elements:** XPRL Reduced Level Excavation; XPSC Site Clearance; XPDM Demolition; XPGR Site Grading.

### 33 XR — Roads, Footpaths and Paved Areas

**INCLUDES:** roads, car parks, driveways, footpaths, paved external areas, kerbs, gutters, line marking, road symbols, traffic signs.

### 34 XN — Boundary Walls, Fencing and Gates

**INCLUDES:** all external/site boundary walls, fencing (timber, wire mesh, chain wire, security), gates, retaining walls forming boundary.

### 35 XB — Outbuildings and Covered Ways

**INCLUDES:** ancillary buildings <200 m² GFA (per Vol 1 §2.2 separate buildings rule, may be reported in element XB); attached or independent covered ways between buildings; pergolas, gazebos.

**Note:** Ancillary buildings containing functional area accommodation are NOT site works — they're separate buildings with their own elements 01–29 (Vol 1 §2.1).

**Note:** Covered ways and bridges between main buildings (Travel function) treated as Project Specifics.

### 36 XL — Landscaping and Improvements

**INCLUDES:** topsoil; cultivation; seeding; turfing; mulching; trees and shrubs (incl. planting); maintenance during defects period; irrigation systems; garden walls; pools/ponds; landscape lighting; site furniture (benches, bins, signs).

## EXTERNAL SERVICES (37–44)

### 37 XK — External Stormwater Drainage

**INCLUDES:** all external stormwater drainage — pits, manholes, pipework, headwalls, soakaway drains, agricultural drains, connections to street/public stormwater.

### 38 XD — External Sewer Drainage

**INCLUDES:** sewer pipework, pits, traps, manholes, septic tanks (if applicable), connections to street/public sewer.

### 39 XW — External Water Supply

**INCLUDES:** external water supply mains, hydrants, valves, meters, connection to public water main.

### 40 XG — External Gas

**INCLUDES:** external gas mains, valves, meters, connection to public gas main.

### 41 XF — External Fire Protection

**INCLUDES:** external fire mains, hydrants, hose reels, fire booster connections, foam systems, water supply tanks for fire.

### 42 XE — External Electric Light and Power

**INCLUDES:** external mains, sub-mains, transformers, switchgear, external switchboards, external lighting (street, area, security), connection to grid, external bus ducts, cable trays.

### 43 XC — External Communications

**INCLUDES:** external telephone, data, communications cabling/conduits, antennas, dish-mounted equipment, connection to public exchange, external sound distribution, external CCTV.

### 44 XS — External Special Services

**INCLUDES:** external special-purpose services (e.g. process supply mains, custom incineration plant, district cooling, district heating where centralised).

---

## 45 XX — External Alterations and Renovations

**Definition:** Alterations and renovations to **external structures or services**.

**Use:** When the project includes modifications to existing site infrastructure (e.g. extending an existing car park, modifying existing site drainage). Treated as Project Specific per Vol 1 §2.1.

---

## 46 YY — Special Provisions

**Definition:** Items not directly part of the building cost but contributing to **Gross Project Cost (GPC)**.

**INCLUDES:**
- (a) Contract Contingency
- (b) Fees (professional consultants, statutory fees)
- (c) Design Contingency (during pre-construction stages)
- (d) Escalation / Rise and Fall (forecast cost adjustment)
- (e) Loose Furniture and Equipment (not part of main contract)
- (f) Allowances for Operational Maintenance (where applicable)
- (g) Locality and time index adjustments

**Excluded from Net Project Cost (NPC).**

### Reconciliation

| Sum | Includes | Excludes |
|---|---|---|
| **Net Project Cost (NPC)** | Elements 01–29 for each building, plus 30 CE, 31 AR, 32–45 external | 46 YY Special Provisions |
| **Gross Project Cost (GPC)** | NPC + 46 YY Special Provisions | — |

The Stage A Initial Budget structure (per Vol 1 §2.1) includes Special Provisions explicitly: "Special Provisions" → element YY → contract contingency, rise and fall, fees, loose furniture and equipment.

### Sub-elements of 46 YY

Typical sub-element divisions:
- YYCC Contract Contingency
- YYDC Design Contingency
- YYRF Rise and Fall (Cost Adjustment)
- YYPF Professional Fees
- YYLF Loose Furniture and Equipment
- YYSP Special Provisions Other (e.g. art, statutory fees beyond approval, special insurance)

---

## When to apply this skill

When: allocating site works, external works, or external services to their X-prefix elemental codes; reconciling Net Project Cost vs Gross Project Cost; deciding what's "internal" vs "external" alterations; identifying which contingency goes under YY vs in a building element; handling a project with multiple buildings on one site (groups 32–45 are at total-project level); deciding whether a covered way is XB Outbuildings or a Project Specific (Travel Engineering Area function); allocating professional fees and loose furniture to YY rather than a building.
