# ANZSMM 2018 — Substructure (§4–11)

Inherits all general rules from `anzsmm-general`. Covers everything from site clearance to the underside of the superstructure: groundworks, piling, in-situ and precast concrete, formwork, reinforcement, prestressing, and tanking/waterproof membranes.

---

## §4 GROUNDWORKS

### §4.1 Excavation

**Items always given:**
- (a) samples
- (b) tests
- (c) keeping excavations free from rain/percolating water (state if below normal water level; state water level, tidal levels)
- Excavations for services → included with respective items in other Sections (note cross-references)

**Classification & units:**

| Class | Units | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Siteworks — clearing site | m² | State trees ≤0.5m girth (with stumps and roots) |
| Siteworks — removal of trees | no | >0.5m girth in 0.5m increments |
| Protection/retention of existing trees and shrubs | no/item | |
| Turf or vegetable soil to be stockpiled | m² | State average depth |
| Poisoning of tree suckers/roots | item | |
| Poisoning/treatment of general ground areas, sides/bottom of trenches | m² | M4: measure as one item |
| 2. Surface excavation to reduce levels | m³ | State area |
| 3. Basement and similar excavations | m³ | 1m, >1m–2m, then 1m increments. State if preliminary trenching measured separately |
| 4. Pit excavation | m³ | State where ≤1m² on plan and number of pits, depth |
| 5. Trench excavation (strip footings, ground beams, similar) | m³ | Rat baffle/kerbing in m |
| 6. Backfill | m³ | 1m, >1m–2m, then 1m increments. Trench backfill measured separately (1m deep, 0.5m diameter increments) |
| 7. Disposal of surplus excavated materials | m³ | State approx distance to nominated disposal area in km |
| 8. Bored excavation (post holes, pier holes, similar) | no / m³ | Per cast in-situ bored piling rules (§5.2) |
| 10. Tunneling | — | State length, width, height; describe method of execution |
| 11. Surface preparation (reduce level, floor slab, road pavement, similar) | m² | State if slopes >30° from horizontal |
| 12. Working space and/or maintaining faces | m³ | State total depth range and purpose |
| 13. Designed shoring | — | Measure where detailed in contract |

**Key measurement rules:**
- **M1**: Measure all excavation and subsequent disposal **before excavation** (i.e. as in-bank volume).
- **M2**: Measure tree girth at 0.5m above ground level.
- **M5**: Measure to the **outside of the footings**.
- **M6**: Measure volume = net area on plan × depth of excavation.
- **M7**: Measure preliminary trenches **separately**.
- **M8**: Service pipe/cable excavation per the appropriate Sections.
- **M13**: Working space = girth on outside line of footings × depth of excavation.
- **M14**: Always measure (incl. rock) **whether required or not**, so risk can be priced.
- **M15**: Make a special note where excavation for working space is not possible.

**Definitions:**
- D2: Total depth = from commencing level to base of excavation in **one item**.
- D3: Preliminary trenches = trenches (e.g. for basement walls) constructed before the basement.
- D4 (surface prep): Levelling, grading, trimming, compacting, scabbling, similar.
- D5: Working space = for formwork, tanking, masonry walls, similar.
- D6: Working space may not be possible (boundary, etc.).

**Deemed included (P1):**
Levelling, grading, trimming, compacting, scabbling and similar to faces of excavations (other than to reduce level, floor slab and road pavement); keeping sides plumb and consolidating.

### §4.2 Filling and Hardcore
- M1: Measure filling/hardcore **after consolidation**.
- M2: Filling to roads → use §37.2 Roadworks.
- Classes: filling in beds, behind retaining walls, over site, to slopes >30° from horizontal, to trenches.
- Units: m³ (1m total depth, then 1m increments) or m² where ≤250mm thick.
- Surface preparation: m² (state slopes >30°).
- D1: Total depth from commencing level to base in one item.

### §4.3 Paper and Plastic Membranes
- Classes: paper, plastic, similar membranes.
- Units: m² horizontal/sloping/vertical (state laps and jointing).
- m for internal angles, external angles, fillets, ≥500mm turn-ups.
- no for around pockets, bonding bricks, bonding blocks, similar.
- M1: Measure net area covered (incl. horizontal breaks ≤500mm and turn-ups ≤500mm).

### §4.4 Underpinning
- Describe work to be underpinned (length, depth, sequence and limit of length in one operation).
- Provide and fix temporary supports (give particulars).
- Classes: excavation to provide access; excavation directly below base; maintaining faces; cutting away old concrete/foundations; cutting away isolated footings; concrete/brickwork in underpinning; grouting; designed shoring.
- M3: Measure 1.5m from face of work being underpinned, from commencing level down to bottom of underpinning.

### §4.5 Rock Stabilization
- (a) Submission of design data.
- Classes: drilling for rock bolts, bolts, rock anchors, ground anchors, grouting, tightening, rock bolts/anchors to specified capacities.
- Units: m for drilling/grouting (state diameter, no. of holes); no for bolts/anchors (state type, length 250mm increments, diameter, capacity, treatment to ends).

## §5 PILING

**Always give:**
- (a) Establishing piling equipment on site
- (b) Removal of piling equipment from site on completion
- (c) Test loading
- (d) Trial piles

**M1**: Give piles separately from each level (where driven from different working levels — include commencing level in description).

### §5.1 Driven Piling

| Class | Units |
|---|---|
| 1. Setting up piling rig | no |
| Fabrication or preparation, supply, handling and pitching | no — state size/capacity, dimensions, protective measures, splice details, length |
| Driving piles | m |
| Cutting off any excess length | no |
| 2. Sheet piling — supply, handling, pitching, driving | m² (overall plan length or girth × depth) |
| Sheet piling — extracting | m² |
| Cutting off / splicing | m |

- **M2**: Measure length from toe to underside of pile cap (or cut-off level) plus 1m allowance for driving and cutting waste.
- **M3**: Group timber piles >5m in 1m increments.
- **M4**: Group other piles >10m in 2m increments.
- **M5**: Measure from toe pitched to designed level of toe when driven.
- **M8**: Sheet piling = net area placed in position, toe to cut-off/finished level.
- **M9**: No allowance for corrugated/grooved/ribbed/irregular surfaces.

### §5.2 Cast In-Situ Piling

**Driven concrete piling:**
- 1. Setting up piling rig (no)
- 2. Piles (m) — state size/capacity; **include reinforcement**, concrete, provision/driving/extraction of temporary casing, average cut-off length, no. of piles
- 3. Permanent liners (m)
- 4. Forming special bases (no)
- 5. Trimming tops of piles
- 6. Extra reinforcement protruding beyond finished level

- **M2**: Measure as driven depth of casing from commencing level to bottom of casing when driven.

**Bored concrete piling:**
- 1. Setting up rig (no, M6 = each pile diameter)
- 2. Boring for pile holes (m) — state diameter
- 3. Permanent liners (no)
- 4. Under-reaming bored holes (no — state increase in diameter)
- 5. Socketing in rock
- 6. Cleaning out bottom of holes
- 7. Testing bottom of holes
- 8. Concrete (per §6)
- 9. Reinforcement (per §8)

- **M9**: Measure concrete & reinforcement per §6 and §8.

## §6 CONCRETE (§6.1 In-Situ Concrete)

**Items always given:**
- (a) Samples of materials/finishes
- (b) Testing of materials, concrete specimens, similar
- (c) Protecting and curing — state total areas of formed surfaces and slabs on ground

**Key measurement rules:**
- **M1**: Make **NO deductions** for: volume of reinforcement or structural steel — except boxed, rectangular, square hollow section or circular hollow members.
- **M2**: Classify concrete in m³ separately by levels; group consecutive floors having similar floor plan area and form.
- **M3**: Measure reinforced and plain concrete **separately** for each mix or strength.
- **M4–M5**: Describe concrete as compacted, sprayed, or placed by particular method/sequence. Sprayed concrete in m² stating area and finish.
- **M6**: Describe whether placed into formwork, steelwork, or between excavated faces.

**Classification & units:**

| Class | Units | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Footings (bases, pile caps, pad footings, strip footings, ground beams) | m³ | ≤1m² on plan / >1m² on plan |
| 2. Slabs | m³ | 100mm thickness stages stating area in m². Measure over all beams/walls/thickenings/bearings (M8). Slabs deemed horizontal unless classified (M7) |
| 3. Waffle/similar slabs | m³ | |
| 4. External pavings | m³ | Surfaces graded to falls/cross-falls; slopes ≤15° / >15° / permanently cambered |
| 5. Roads | m³ | |
| 6. Walls | m³ | 50mm thickness stages stating area in m². Including columns/piers attached to and projecting from walls |
| 7. Columns and beams (isolated/attached) | m³ | Upstand beams: measure above top of slabs (M9) |
| 8. Casing to steelwork | m³ | |
| 9. Stairs | m³ | Including landings, strings, beams |
| 10. Copings, kerbs, urinal steps, similar | m | State length |
| 11. Plinths, plant and machine bases | (group together, M11) | |
| 12. Ducts, channels, box drains | m³ if >250mm internal width/depth (M12); m if ≤250mm and isolated (M13) | |
| 13. Bearings (bearing blocks, similar isolated) | no | M14: measure when ≤0.25m² |
| 14. Reglets | m | |
| 15. Expansion and similar joints | m | ≤250mm wide. Includes preparation, formwork, waterstops, accessories |
| 16. Scabbling | m² | M18: only re. base/top/face/end of walls; base of stairs; base/top/face of columns; top of beams |
| 17. Cutting concrete | m for cutting; no for drilling |
| 18. Kerbs, gutters, etc | m (state expansion joint details and spacing) — kerbs, margins, edging strips, spoon gutters, combined kerbs and gutters; openings for sumps in no |
| 19. Blinding | m² horizontal/sloping/vertical (state thickness; D2: 0–15° horizontal, D3: 15°–75° sloping, D4: >75° vertical) |
| 20. Integral finishes | m² (or m if ≤250mm wide) — wood floated, rubber floated, steel trowelled, similar |
| 21. Surface hammering and blasting | m² (or m if ≤250mm wide) — classify as for formwork (M22) |

**Deemed included (P1):**
Placing in position; making good after removal of formwork and casting in all required items; additional concrete to conform to structural & excavated tolerances; pour joints and joints shown on drawings that don't require formwork; scabbling other than that required in classification 16.

## §7 FORMWORK

### §7.1 Formwork (general)

**Items given:**
- (a) Engineer Certification of formal design
- (b) Preparation of shop drawings

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: Describe formwork left in position.
- **M2**: Measure in **m where ≤250mm wide** (other than columns/beams and narrow widths caused by openings).
- **M3**: Measure separately formwork producing permanent finish or specified concrete surface.
- **M4**: Measure to the **net contact surface** between concrete and formwork.
- **M5**: Classify by levels; group consecutive similar floors.
- **M6**: Measure separately for each class of formwork.
- **M7**: Fluted/specified finish formwork — measure flat overall area, NOT net contact.

**Classification & units:**

| Class | Units | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Face of footing | m² | Partially above / wholly in ground. M8: full height to sides. M9: only where expressly directed |
| 2. Soffits of slabs | m² | Horizontal / sloping ≤15° / >15° / permanently cambered. State if slab >200mm thick or over shafts. Including strutting up to 3m, then 1m increments. State min/max heights |
| 3–6. Faces of walls (incl. retaining, core, inside faces of shaft core walls) | m² | M10: measure separately walls formed on one face only |
| 7–8. Sides of columns / piers | m² (m for circular, stating diameter) | Isolated / attached. M11: no deduction where intersected by beams. State sectional shape |
| 9–10. Sides and soffits of beams / lintels | m² | State if beam >750mm deep. M12: measure up to sides of columns/walls. M13: secondary beams up to main beams (no deduction) |
| 11. Soffits of stair flights | m² | |
| 12. Edges (slabs, walls, similar) | m² | |
| 13. Steps | m² | |
| 14. Top formwork | m² | Sloping >15° from horizontal |
| 15. Surfaces of cornices | m² | |
| 16. Surfaces of mouldings | m² | |
| 17. Waffle form or similar system (permanent / temporary / supports and decking) | no / m² | M14: measure area of structure to be supported over all associated beams in same plane |
| 18. Penetrations (walls/slabs/similar) | no | ≤1m². Rectangular: 0.25m² increments; circular: 100mm diameter increments ≤200mm; state size >200mm diameter |
| 19. Recesses / 20. similar items | no | ≤1m² |
| 21. Formation (chases, grooves, rebates) | m | ≤50mm in either dimension; state size if >50mm |
| 22. Chamfers (>50mm ≤250mm wide) — beams, columns, similar | m | |
| 23. Splayed internal angles | m | |
| 24. Raking cutting / 25. Circular cutting | m | |

**Deemed included (P1):**
All necessary boarding, supports, erecting, framing, temporary cambering, cutting, perforations for reinforcing bars/bolts/straps/ties/hangers/pipes, angles, removal, cleaning, wetting, treatment with separating agent, chamfered edges and splayed internal angles ≤50mm wide on face, filling tie bolt holes, strutting left after removal.

### §7.2 Permanent Metal Formwork System
- State type of formwork.
- Classes mirror §7.1 (soffits of slabs, faces of walls, sides of columns/piers, sides/soffits of beams/lintels, etc.) plus:
  - 13–15. Special starter panels / closer panels / closing ends of panels
  - 19. Welding (ends/intermediate bearings of panels to supporting structure; welding flanges in 'ribs down' construction) — no, state dimensions, number, length, centres of welds or pin diameter; whether decking is 'ribs up' or 'ribs down'
  - 20. Power fixing
  - 21. Shear connectors — no, state diameter and length
- D2: Panels at edges of formwork. D3: Penetrations for ductwork, access hatches and similar.

### §7.3 Slip Form Construction
- (a) Bending reinforcement for casting into subsequent construction
- (b) Provision and setting up the system
- (c) Adjustment at change points (differing wall thicknesses or plan arrangements)
- (d) Subsequent dismantling and removal
- **M1**: Concrete, formwork, reinforcement and similar measured separately per the applicable rules unless otherwise provided.
- **M2**: Measure to all faces over all openings.
- Classes: slip formwork (m²), blockouts (no, 0.25m² increments), integral surface finishes (inner face when ≤1m², each size when >1m², m²).

## §8 REINFORCEMENT

**Items given:**
- (a) Bending schedules where not supplied free to builder, stating quantities of all reinforcement

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: Classify by levels; group consecutive similar floors.
- **M2**: Classify reinforcement between splice points in columns/walls; if not in same height stages, state locations of splice points relative to floor levels.
- **M3**: Measure separately special labours/treatments (welding, bitumen dipping, similar — D1).
- **M4**: Where not documented to design lengths, **make no allowance for laps** and state this.
- **M5**: Calculate mass = **net theoretical mass** with no allowance for rolling margin.
- **M6**: Measure laps as shown on drawings.
- **M7**: Reinforcement in string courses, copings, kerbs, plinths and similar — include with the classification item to which they're attached.

**Classification & units:**

| Class | Units | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Bars (in footings, slabs, walls/retaining/core, columns, beams, casing to steelwork, stairs/landings, external pavings) | t | State type; if galvanised. Bars >10m and ≤12m long, then 2m increments. Group ≤16mm sides/diameter (state sizes); group >16mm and ≤36mm; state separately each side/diameter >36mm |
| 2. Bars as helical reinforcement | t | |
| 3. Bars as ties (D2: links, spacers, stirrups, bindings, similar) | t | |
| 4. Fabric | m² | |
| 5. Trench mesh | m | State width |
| 6. Special clamps | no | State size; required treatment to ends of bars |

**Deemed included (P1):**
Cleaning, fabrication, placing, all necessary temporary fixings and supports (tie wire, chair supports), rolling margins, **laps other than laps shown on drawings**.

## §9 PRESTRESSING

**Items given:** (a) supply of expendable items.

**Key rules:**
- **M1**: Measure separately the various methods of tensioning.
- **M2**: Measure separately concrete, formwork and reinforcement to prestressed concrete elements.
- **M3**: Measure separately for straight and curved sleeves or sheathing.
- **M4**: Calculate mass on the length between anchorages.

**Classification:**

| Class | Units |
|---|---|
| 1. Cores or ducts (forming and grouting/sealing) | m (≤5m long, then 2m increments) — state size, no., sleeve/sheathing details, grout |
| 2. Filling recesses and shear keys (jacking/anchor recesses, shear keys) | no |
| 3. Stressing steel — supply and delivery | t — state diameter, lengths, number of wires per cable, type of central core/sheathing, ultimate strength |
| Degreasing, straightening, cutting, assembling, hoisting, fixing | no — state diameter, including cutting ends and waste |
| 4. Dead anchorages | no — state diameter |
| 5. Live anchorages | no |
| 6. Tensioning of stressing steel cables | item |

**D1:** Expendable items — cones, wedges, anchor plates, spacers, similar.

**Deemed included (P1):** Formation and grouting of air holes.

## When to apply this skill

Apply when the BOQ includes any of: site clearance, excavation (basement/pit/trench/surface), filling, hardcore, membranes (paper/plastic/asphalt/bitumen/roll-on/trowel-on), underpinning, rock stabilization, piling (driven/cast in-situ/bored/sheet), in-situ concrete (footings, slabs, walls, columns, beams, stairs, kerbs, pavings, blinding, integral finishes), formwork (any form), reinforcement (bars/fabric/mesh/clamps), prestressing, precast concrete elements, tanking/waterproof membranes.
