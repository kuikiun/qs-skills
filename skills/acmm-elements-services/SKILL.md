This skill covers the Services and Centralised Energy element groups. Element costs include supply, installation and all necessary work for satisfactory operation of equipment — including freight, insurance, travelling time, profit, testing, commissioning, working drawings, manuals, "as installed" drawings.

For Substructure/Superstructure see `acmm-elements-substructure-superstructure`. For finishes/fittings see `acmm-elements-finishes-fittings`. For external/special see `acmm-elements-external-and-special`.

## 17 SF — Sanitary Fixtures

**Definition:** Fit out with normal fixtures connected to soil + waste plumbing systems and associated ancillaries.

**INCLUDES:** WC suites; urinals; basins; sinks and tubs; troughs and runnels; drinking fountains; slop hoppers; showers; hobs; shower curtains and trays; terminal outlets integral with fixtures; flusherette valves; soap and toilet paper holders; towel rails; hand dryers.

**EXCLUDES:** sanitary macerators; bed pan washers; kitchen, laundry and sterilizing equipment, refrigerated drinking water coolers (SE); sanitary incinerators (SS); floor wastes and all loose traps (PD); terminal outlets not integral with fixtures (WS).

**Unit:** No. per SF. SF = sum of SFCA to SFSH + any in SFNE (excludes valves and outlets). **Fixture Units** per National Plumbing Code.

## 18 PD — Sanitary Plumbing

**Definition:** Soil + waste pipework. INCLUDES vent pipes; floor wastes; loose traps; pipework and fittings to fixtures.

Sub-elements typically:
- PDSP Soil/Vent Pipes
- PDWP Waste Pipes
- PDFW Floor Wastes
- PDTP Loose Traps
- PDNE Not Elsewhere

---

## 19 WS — Water Supply

**INCLUDES:** cold water pipework; non-refrigerated drinking water; central refrigerated drinking; flusherette services; HW pipework. Sub-elements typically:
- WSMS Cold Water Mains, Sub-mains
- WSCO Cold Water Outlets
- WSHW Hot Water (storage units, reticulation)
- WSTW Treated Water
- WSTO Terminal Outlets
- WSNE Not Elsewhere

## 20 GS — Gas Service

**INCLUDES:** gas mains, sub-mains, terminal outlets within building; meters; valves.

## 21 SH — Space Heating

**INCLUDES:** central plant for non-AC heating (boilers, calorifiers); reticulation; radiators, convectors, unit heaters, radiant panels; controls; electric panel/floor heating; gas appliances; controls; insulation.

---

## 22 VE — Ventilation

**INCLUDES:** mechanical supply and exhaust ventilation; ductwork; fans; grilles; filters; controls. Excludes air conditioning (AC) and evaporative cooling (EC).

---

## 23 EC — Evaporative Cooling

**INCLUDES:** evaporative cooling plant + reticulation, ductwork, fans, grilles, controls.

---

## 24 AC — Air Conditioning

**INCLUDES:** air-conditioning plant; refrigeration; ductwork; air handling; chilled/heated water systems; reticulation; controls; insulation; commissioning. Sub-elements typically use prefixes ACMZ, ACFC, ACRP, ACBL, ACRE, ACAC, ACEW, ACCT, ACNE.

---

## 25 FP — Fire Protection

**INCLUDES:** fire hydrants; hose reels; sprinklers; fire extinguishers; fire detection and alarm; fire blankets; pumps; reticulation. Often shared with electrical (alarms) — early agreement on responsibility needed.

---

## 26 LP — Electric Light and Power

**INCLUDES:** mains, sub-mains, switchboards, distribution boards; lighting (incl. emergency); power outlets; final sub-circuits; light fittings and lamps; switches and outlets; bus ducts; conduits and cables; cable carriers; earthing; lightning conductors; identification (colour bands, tags, plates); marking; tools and spares.

---

## 27 CM — Communications

**INCLUDES:** telephones (PABX, lines); paging; intercom; PA; TV; sound distribution; signals; clocks; bells; nurse call; security systems; data; cabling; outlets.

---

## 28 TS — Transportation Systems

**INCLUDES:** lifts (passenger/goods/service); escalators; travelators; dumb-waiters; conveyor systems; pneumatic tubes; hoists; chutes; vacuum systems associated with cleaning. **Excludes:** lift shafts (SB if below ground floor; UF/RF for shaft walls); lift motor rooms (E5 engineering area).

---

## 29 SS — Special Services

**INCLUDES:** services of a specialised nature not covered elsewhere — process cooling; cool rooms; vacuum systems for medical/dental; reticulated medical gases; sterile water; clean steam; pneumatic tube systems; security/audio-visual cabling; reticulated compressed air; specialised process services.

---

## 30 CE — Centralised Energy Systems

**Definition:** Plant + reticulation supplying **thermal and/or site-generated electrical energy to a number of buildings** and/or multiple energy-consuming elements within a single building.

**Allocated to total project, NOT individual buildings.** Cost of all CE plant + reticulation + plant room areas → CE element. CE plant rooms → "Centralised Energy Area" functional area.

**Sub-elements** typically include:
- CE Plant (boilers, chillers, generators, fuel storage)
- CE Reticulation (steam, hot water, chilled water mains, fuel mains, electrical mains)
- CE Plant Buildings (where structurally separate)
- CEPB CE Plant Buildings code

CE rates **excluded from** elemental and functional area rates for individual buildings — facilitates budgeting of staged projects (Vol 1 §2.1).

## Cost handling rules across services elements

### Element costs include
Supply, installation, all necessary work for satisfactory operation. Freight, insurance, travelling time, profit, testing, commissioning, working drawings, manuals, "as installed" drawings.

### Sharing elements between disciplines
Some elements (e.g. Fire Protection) shared between mechanical and electrical. Early team agreement on responsibility is essential. Use the element/sub-element list as a "shopping list" check.

### Engineer's responsibility
Each engineer is responsible for costing those elements relative to their specific discipline.

### Schedule of prices format
For all significant projects, include a **schedule of prices** in tender documents matching the element/sub-element structure (e.g. ACMZ01, ACMZ02, ACFC03, ACRP02, ACBL, ACRE, ACAC, ACEW, ACCT, ACNE, VEDS, VEDE, WSHW, WSNE — sample from Vol 1 Figure 4 Police Station and Court House).

### Element cost storage units
- For project summary: GFA
- For individual sub-element: specific unit per Vol 2 (e.g. $/point, $/L/s, $/kW, $/m, $/no.)
- Multiple units listed → store quantities in as many units as possible
